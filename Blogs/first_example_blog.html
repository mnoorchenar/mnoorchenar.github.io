The bias-variance tradeoff is one of the most fundamental concepts in machine learning, yet it's often misunderstood or oversimplified. This interactive post uses a dartboard analogy to help you visualize and understand how bias and variance affect model performance, and why finding the right balance is crucial for building effective predictive models.

What Are Bias and Variance?

Imagine you're playing darts, aiming for the bullseye. In machine learning terms:

Bias represents how far off your average shot is from the target. High bias means your shots consistently miss the bullseye in the same direction.

Variance represents how scattered your shots are. High variance means your shots are spread out, even if they might average close to the target.
